Tamales

Traditional Mexican Tamales

Tamales are a unique Pre-Columbian dish that are believed to have originated in between North and South America. Mexican tamales being the best-known version. Although almost all Central and South American cultures have their own version.

Tamales are a complete meal wrapped up in a corn husk, in a portable form. The flavor comes from a combination of dried chilies and spices that are rubbed and coat the pork shoulder meat. The pork is then cooked till the meat is soft, put into the middle of the masa, and then steamed till cooked.

Ingredients

  • 1 small onion, finely chopped
  • 5 large ancho chiles, stems and seeds removed
  • 2 morita chiles
  • coriander, and cumin seeds
  • 1 pound pork shoulder
  • salt and pepper
  • 3 pounds of masa

Steps

  1. Cook onions, until golden brown and softened. Add chillies and broth and bring to a boil. Cover pot, remove from heat, and let sit 30 minutes.
  2. Preheat oven to 250 degrees. Transfer chile mixture to a blender. Add spices and blend. Place pork shoulder in a pot and cover with chile puree. Bring to a boil, coverpot, and transfer to oven.
  3. Prepare your pre made masa, and corn husks. Start to spread masa on the corn husks and then fill with the pork filling. Wrap the tamales and set aside till finished.
  4. Put all the made tamales into the steamer for an hour and start to check them to see if they're ready to eat.
